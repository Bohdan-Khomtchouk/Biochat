TITLE
Gut microbiota regulate xenobiotic metabolism in the liver

ORGANISM
Mus musculus

SUMMARY
Germ-free (GF) mice can be used as a powerful tool to investigate the role of the intestinal commensal flora in metabolism and immune tolerance. In this study we have used whole genome transcriptome analyses to compare expression levels systemically in liver of germ-free mice and specific pathogen free (SPF) mice. DESIGN
Two-condition experiment, GF mice liver vs. SPF mice liver. Biological replicates: 4 GF mice liver, 4 SPF mice liver. Each replicate was hybridized on 2 arrays with dyeswap. GSMxxx.gpr test is Cy5. GSMxxxdyeswap.gpr test is Cy3.
